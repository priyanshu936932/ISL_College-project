How the AI Understands Your Hands

A deep dive into the technology powering instant gesture recognition

🔄 The Recognition Pipeline

1
📹

Video Upload

You upload an MP4, AVI, or MOV video containing your ISL gesture (2-3 seconds)

2
🔢

Frame Extraction

OpenCV extracts all frames from the video at 30fps or higher

3
📐

Preprocessing

Frames are intelligently downsampled to exactly 16 frames and resized to 112×112 pixels

4
🧠

AI Analysis

R(2+1)D-18 neural network analyzes the video in one forward pass

5
📊

Softmax

Network outputs are normalized using softmax to get probabilities for all 7 classes

6
🏆

Prediction

The class with highest probability is returned as the prediction with confidence score

🔬 The AI Model

🧠 R(2+1)D-18 Architecture

A 3D Convolutional Neural Network that processes video sequences as a sequence of 2D spatial frames plus temporal information. The model is pretrained on the Kinetics-400 dataset (400,000 videos of human actions) and fine-tuned on our custom ISL gesture dataset.

  • ✅ Pretrained on Kinetics-400 (400K videos)
  • ✅ Fine-tuned on ISL dataset (7 gestures)
  • ✅ Optimized for temporal patterns

📸 Frame Processing

Raw RGB video frames are the input to the model, not hand landmarks. This allows the network to learn features like hand shape, finger positions, motion dynamics, and body posture together.

  • ✅ Input: 16 frames × 112×112 pixels
  • ✅ Format: RGB color (3 channels)
  • ✅ Normalization: 0-1 float values

🎯 Training & Accuracy

Trained with data augmentation (flips, rotations, brightness changes) to improve robustness. Achieves 96% accuracy on the test set across all 7 ISL gesture classes.

  • ✅ 96% Training Accuracy
  • ✅ Data Augmentation Applied
  • ✅ 7-Class Classification

💡 Tips for Best Results

💡

Good Lighting

Ensure your hands and face are well-lit. Natural lighting or bright room lighting works best. Avoid shadows on your hands.

🎬

Plain Background

Use a contrasting background (white wall, solid color) to help the model focus on your hands and body movements.

📏

Distance from Camera

Position yourself 2-3 feet from the camera so your entire upper body (shoulders, arms, hands) is visible in the frame.

👐

Visible Hands

Keep both hands visible throughout the gesture. The model learns from hand position, shape, and motion patterns.

⚡

Natural Pace

Perform the gesture at a natural, deliberate pace (not too fast, not too slow). This matches how the model was trained.

⏱️

Video Length

Record 2-3 seconds of video per gesture. The model automatically extracts 16 evenly-spaced frames for consistent analysis.
