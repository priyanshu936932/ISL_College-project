Why India Needs This

63 million people in India are deaf or hard of hearing. But only 1% of India knows ISL.

🚨 The Problem

👥

63 Million People

India has 63 million people who are deaf or hard of hearing — that's about 5% of India's population. Yet the hearing majority knows almost nothing about sign language.

🌐

Communication Gap

Less than 1% of India's hearing population knows Indian Sign Language. This creates a massive communication barrier in schools, hospitals, workplaces, and government offices.

📚

No Standard Curriculum

There is no standardized curriculum for learning ISL. No consistent sign dictionary. This makes sign language education fragmented and inaccessible.

👨‍💼

Only 300 Interpreters

India has only ~300 certified ISL interpreters for a country of 1.4 billion people. Professional sign language support is almost non-existent.

🏫

Education Gap

Most deaf children lack proper ISL education. Without early exposure to sign language, they face delays in language development and cognitive skills.

💼

Employment Challenges

Deaf individuals face discrimination and lack of accessibility in the job market. Many employers are unaware of ISL or accommodations needed.

🚀 Our Mission

🌉

Bridge the Communication Gap

Democratize sign language recognition so deaf and hearing people can communicate more easily, in schools, hospitals, offices, and every part of society.

🤖

Empower with AI

Leverage AI to make ISL recognition instant, accessible, and affordable. No expensive interpreters needed — the technology is available to everyone.

📱

Make It Accessible

Create tools that work for deaf individuals, educators, healthcare workers, and developers. Our app is free, open, and built with inclusivity in mind.
